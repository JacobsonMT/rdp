The registry collects information on model organism researchers and the specific genes they study. The registry is the mechanism by which the will find potential matches of model organism researchers to human rare disease researchers.

The Registry collects two types of information. First, you will enter your basic contact information and some general information about your research (under "Profile"). Second, you will enter information about genes you study, for one or more model organisms ("Model Organisms"). Obtaining a correct and comprehensive list of genes is of key interest. In order to assist registrants in this regard we devised a three-tier gene model described. Please read carefully before you start adding genes to your profile.

All information in the Registry is confidential and will only be used by the Network administration for matching you to rare disease research projects. Your information will never be shared with any third party (including other registered researchers) without your consent.
